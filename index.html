Overview

Mobile Phone Fines While Driving: Know the Risks

This interactive dashboard provides insights into mobile phone fines while driving across Australian jurisdictions. The three core visualizations allow you to:

  • 1. Fines Trend Analysis - Track fine patterns over time, comparing jurisdictions and detection methods (cameras vs police). See how penalties have evolved annually.
  • 2. Geographic Hotspots - Explore heatmaps showing fine concentrations by location and age group. Identify high-risk areas and demographics.
  • 3. 2023 Enforcement Data - Examine last year's detailed breakdown of fines, charges and arrests by jurisdiction and age group.
Customize views using filters for time periods, locations, and enforcement types. The data helps drivers understand risks while assisting policymakers in evaluating road safety measures.

Fines Over Time

The Rising Tide of Mobile Fines

In just 15 years, mobile phone fines in Australia exploded by 1,700% - from 156,000 (2008) to 2.8 million (2023). What caused this seismic shift? Australia's mobile phone fines evolved through three phases: Stable enforcement (2008-2015, ~150K fines/year) declined slightly (2016-2019) before high-tech detection systems triggered unprecedented growth. Annual fines tripled to 360K+ by 2022, with NSW/QLD now accounting for 80% of all fines. This 1,700% national increase since 2008 demonstrates the effectiveness of modern enforcement - no driver is immune from today's strict detection systems.

    Enforcement Stats

    Who's Getting Caught - And How

    Australia's mobile phone fines reveal stark generational and geographic divides. Drivers aged 26-39 face 41% of all fines nationwide - over 23,000 penalties last year - while representing just 22% of license holders. Victoria dominates with 55% of national fines (31,342), contrasting sharply with Tasmania's 2,428 or the NT's mere 205. Surprisingly, 37 fines targeted under-16s who can't legally drive. This data exposes uncomfortable truths: enforcement disproportionately impacts young urban drivers, with your postcode proving as consequential as your age when it comes to fine risk.
